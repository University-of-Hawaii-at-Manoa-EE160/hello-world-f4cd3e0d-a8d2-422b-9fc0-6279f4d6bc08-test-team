    Fellow Students:

    • Collaboration is encouraged (except on midterms and finals)

    • "If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough."

      Albert Einstein
      • Teaching a fellow student will enforce your own understanding
    • Learning from a fellow student who has just learned a new concept will be easier than learning from someone who has forgotten why this new concept may be troublesome
    • A fellow student who has just learned a concept can explain the concept better because they are aware of the "assumptions" and "gotchas" involved
    • A handful of instructors teaching a class of 70-100 students is not as effective as every student teaching every other student
    • Collaborating does not mean cheating; do collaborate; don't cheat
    • Make sure you understand the material and concepts when collaborating; don't just get the answer

    Fellow Humans:

    • Search online with Google or any other search engine
    • Post a question on question and answer forums, like StackOverflow

  • Grading Policy

    Assignments/Homework:

    • Will count for 0% of final grade
    • Use assignments/homeworks to understand the material for the exams

    Participation:

    • Will be counted as bonus points only if:
      • Student is on the borderline between 2 grade brackets
      • Student is unhappy with his/her final grade and makes a request to apply participation bonus points
    • Has a maximum potential of 5%
    • Based on quantity and quality of questions and answers posted on Piazza

    Midterms/Finals:

    • Will count for 100% of final grade
    • Examination Goals
      Solve ProblemC Programming Language
      Straight Forward Problems Understand the problem Understand C syntax
      Deeper Understanding Problems Engineer an algorithm (or pseudo code) to solve the problem Read and follow a small C program (or small snippets of C code) to determine the program intent and output
      Critical Thinking Problems Write a C program to solve the problem
    • Recovery Assignment
      • At the end of each midterm (ie not final), there will be a recovery assignment
      • Is optional
      • Intended to "recover" some lost points on the midterm
      • Will be submitted individually
      • Is due 2 weeks after the midterm scores are posted, which should be the same time the recovery assignment is posted
      • Has a maximum potential of 10%
      • Will not boost midterm grade past 59%
      • Example Scenarios
        Initial Midterm ScoreRecovery Assignment ScoreFinal Midterm Score
        Scenario 1 30% 10% 40%
        Scenario 2 50% 10% 59%

    Final Grade:

    • Grading Scale
      Weighted AverageGuaranteed Letter Grade
      80% or greaterA
      60% or greaterB
      40% or greaterC
      20% or greaterD
    • Grades in this course will NOT be curved (some scaling of exam scores may be done if necessary)
    • There will be some "gray" area between letter grades, so that two people with the same weighted average may not get the same letter grade
    • Grades in the "gray" area will be determined by effort and trends (improving or declining) over the semester
    • The +/- grading system will be used in this course

  • Expectations

    Expectations for Lecture Hours:

    • Instructor will lay out basic examples of concepts
    • Instructor will answer as much questions as possible and as quickly as possible; it is understood that having as instant a feedback period can facilitate learning

    Expectations for Time Outside of Lecture Hours:

    • Student will build upon basic concepts from lecture hours by completing assignments
    • Assignments will require student to build upon basic concepts, ie. there will be concepts not thoroughly explained during lecture hours
    • Students are expected to post/ask questions about assignments if they get stuck on particulars of the assignment
    • Students are expected to help each other
    • Students are expected to read the given material before lecture
    • Students are expected to do the previous lecture's assignment before lecture as all topics build upon previous topics

    Expectations for Office Hours and Q&A forum:

    • Students are expected to research and try the assignments before posting/asking questions
    • When a student posts/asks questions, it will help the instructor and fellow classmates to provide helpful answers if the student states what they've tried and also to really isolate what they want to ask
    • Usually, when forming the question, the student sometimes should be able to answer the question themselves
    • Students are expected to respect each other's posts and comments; we are all here trying to learn a new difficult concept
